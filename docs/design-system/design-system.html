Dimensions 53

Measurements that define Vibeliner's spatial rhythm. These aren't isolated numbers — they compose into real objects. Each group below shows the object drawn at real scale with the dimensions called out; the singleton grid at the bottom covers everything else.

Badge

A red circle that caps every annotation mark. Its font is sized to read clearly inside the circle at any zoom level.

badgeDiameter 18 pt
Used by: Annotations/Renderers/BadgeRenderer.swift

Crosshair

The purple aiming cross shown under the cursor during capture selection. Thickness and tick length keep it visible without overwhelming the screenshot underneath; 85% opacity lets a hint of the pixel below bleed through.

crosshairOpacity 0.85 α
crosshairThickness 2.3 pt
crosshairTickLength 10 pt
Used by: Capture/CrosshairView.swift

Arrow

The annotation arrow: one diagonal shaft plus a chevron. Chevron length and angle keep the arrowhead visible at long shaft lengths without looking cartoonish; the shared 2.5pt strokeWidth is defined under shape-primitives.

arrowChevronAngle 28 °
arrowChevronLength 12 pt
Used by: Annotations/Renderers/ArrowRenderer.swift

Note pill

The inline annotation chip that holds typed text. Height is tuned for one line of 12pt copy at comfortable vertical padding; corner radius is exactly half the height, so the pill is always fully rounded.

noteCornerRadius 13 pt
noteHeight 26 pt
Used by: Annotations/Renderers/NotePillRenderer.swift, Annotations/Renderers/NotePillView.swift

Dimension label

The small "45 × 28" pill shown beneath a live capture selection. Fixed height keeps vertical alignment stable; horizontal padding and gap keep the label breathing room from the selection edge.

dimensionLabelCornerRadius 5 pt
dimensionLabelGap 10 pt
dimensionLabelHeight 24 pt
dimensionLabelPaddingH 10 pt
Used by: Capture/DimensionLabelView.swift

Freehand

The free-drawn annotation tool. Sample interval controls point density along the stroke; min points gates when a gesture becomes a real mark so accidental clicks don't register. Shares the 2.5pt strokeWidth with other shapes.

freehandMinPoints 3 points
freehandSampleInterval 3 pt
Used by: Annotations/Renderers/FreehandRenderer.swift, Annotations/Tools/FreehandTool.swift

Shape primitives

The measurements shared across line, rectangle, and circle tools. strokeWidth is the single source of truth for every annotation stroke; rectCornerRadius keeps rectangles subtly rounded; minimumSelectionSize stops tiny accidental drags from turning into tokens.

minimumSelectionSize 10 pt
rectCornerRadius 3 pt
strokeWidth 2.5 pt
Used by: Annotations/Renderers/LineRenderer.swift, Annotations/Renderers/RectangleRenderer.swift, Annotations/Renderers/CircleRenderer.swift

Toolbar

The floating editor toolbar. Height and corner radius are set so the toolbar reads as a single capsule; button diameter fits comfortably within the height leaving a 5pt halo, and the 2pt gap gives a visual beat without looking sparse.

toolButtonSize 30 pt
toolbarCornerRadius 20 pt
toolbarHeight 40 pt
toolbarToolButtonGap 2 pt
Used by: Editor/ToolbarView.swift, Editor/ToolButton.swift
